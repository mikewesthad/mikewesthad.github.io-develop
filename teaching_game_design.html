Middle School Game Design (2014)

2014 game design camp at the School of the Art Institute's Middle School Camp (ages 11-13) that I developed in collaboration with Kayla Lewis that teaches games as a form of creative expression, rather than as simple entertainment. Students studied experimental games and made their own experimental games in Stencyl and in HTML5/Javascript.

Over the course of two intensive weeks of 9a-5p days, students planned and realized three games. While all the games were digital, the process for making them was a hybrid of both non-digital and digital techniques, including: sketching, storyboarding, writing, diagramming, simulating, scanning, digital drawing and programming.
